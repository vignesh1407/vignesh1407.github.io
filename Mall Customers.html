Problem Statement:¶

There is mall in specific city that contains information about the customer who are subcribed to the card, they provided their details like annual income, age and gender. The spending scores are obtained by matching with the customer ID and with the customer income with the amount of money they spend in the mall and number of times they visit per week, the values takes between 1 and 100

Mall management need to segement their clients into different segements based on the matrics 1. Annual Income and 2. Spending scores. Management has no idea about the client's segments and even has no idea about the how many segments there would be.

Solution:¶

Thus this is typically a clustering problem since we can't predict the outcome, so we need to use unsupervised algorithm. Best and easy algorithm are K-Means clustering algorithm

#By using WCSS we are going to iterate for 10 values
wcss=[]
for i in range(1,11):
    #init- Initialization method for choosing centeroids by using K-Means++ method
    #max_iter- Is the maximun number of iteration for finding the final clusters to default 300
    #n_init- # of times the iterations should take place with different centeroids
    #randon_state- Fixes all random factors of Kmeans process so when we set to 0 we will get same results
    kmeans= KMeans(n_clusters=i,init='k-means++',max_iter=300, n_init=10,random_state=0)
    kmeans.fit(X)
    #inertia- is used for math calculation like sum of squares
    wcss.append(kmeans.inertia_)
#Ploting the Elbow method
plt.plot(range(1,11),wcss)
plt.title('Elbow Method')
plt.xlabel("Number of clusters")
plt.ylabel("WCSS")
plt.show()
